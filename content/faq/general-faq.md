## How long does it take to register a company in Cambodia?

A standard company registration with the Ministry of Commerce typically takes 7–14 business days, provided all required documents are prepared and apostilled where necessary. After MOC registration, additional steps including tax registration with the GDT and NSSF enrollment add approximately another 5–10 business days. EP Partners manages this entire process for clients and typically completes full setup within 3–4 weeks.

## What is the minimum capital requirement for a foreign-owned company in Cambodia?

Cambodia does not impose a general minimum capital requirement for most sectors. However, certain regulated industries — banking, insurance, microfinance, and others — have specific minimum registered capital requirements set by their respective regulators. For standard trading, consulting, and service companies, the registered capital is set by the shareholders and is commonly set at USD 10,000–100,000 depending on operational needs.

## Can foreigners own 100% of a Cambodian company?

Yes, foreigners can own 100% of shares in a Cambodian company in most business sectors. The primary restriction relates to land: foreign entities cannot own land in Cambodia directly. Businesses requiring land use typically structure through long-term leases (up to 50 years, renewable), co-ownership with a Cambodian national, or a concession agreement.

## What taxes does a Cambodian company need to pay?

A typical company operating under the Real Regime tax system is subject to: Value Added Tax (VAT) at 10%, monthly Tax on Income (ToI) prepayment at 1% of turnover, annual Tax on Income at 20% of net profit, Withholding Tax on applicable payments, and Patent Tax (annual business license fee). Payroll-related taxes include NSSF contributions for all employees.
