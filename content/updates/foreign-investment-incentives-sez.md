## Why Cambodia's SEZs Are Attracting Attention

Cambodia has positioned itself as one of Southeast Asia's most investor-friendly destinations, particularly through its network of Special Economic Zones (SEZs) and the Qualified Investment Project (QIP) framework administered by the Council for the Development of Cambodia (CDC).

For manufacturers, exporters, and regional business hubs, the combination of low labor costs, duty-free access to major export markets under ASEAN agreements, and substantial tax incentives makes Cambodia a compelling location for expansion.

## Understanding the QIP Framework

A Qualified Investment Project is an investment approved by the CDC that qualifies for tax incentives under Cambodia's Law on Investment. There are two main types of incentive:

### Income Tax Holidays

A QIP may receive a complete exemption from Tax on Income for a period of three to nine years, depending on the nature and scale of the project and the sector in which it operates. The tax holiday begins from the first profit-making year.

After the tax holiday expires, a reduced 20% Tax on Income rate applies — the standard rate for all Cambodian companies.

> For capital-intensive projects that typically take several years to generate profits, the three-year "trigger period" (before the holiday starts running) means the effective tax-free period can extend substantially beyond the stated holiday length.

### Special Depreciation

As an alternative to the income tax holiday, QIPs may elect a 40% special depreciation allowance on new or used tangible properties in the first year of use. This benefits projects that generate immediate profits but prefer to accelerate cost recovery rather than defer tax.

## Customs Duty Exemptions

All QIPs are eligible for an exemption from import duties on:

- Production equipment and construction materials
- Raw materials and semi-finished goods used directly in production

This duty exemption is a significant benefit for import-dependent manufacturers and can reduce capital setup costs substantially.

## Special Economic Zones

Cambodia has developed more than 30 licensed SEZs located across the country, with major zones in Phnom Penh, Sihanoukville, Kampot, and border areas with Thailand and Vietnam.

### Benefits of Locating Inside an SEZ

- One-stop service for government approvals and permits
- Dedicated customs clearance procedures
- Built-in infrastructure (power, water, broadband, warehousing)
- Fast-track QIP approval processing

> Companies inside SEZs still need to apply separately for QIP status to access income tax holidays — SEZ location alone does not automatically confer QIP tax incentives.

## Eligible Investment Sectors

Priority sectors for QIP approval typically include:

- Manufacturing for export (garments, footwear, electronics)
- Agro-processing and food production
- Tourism infrastructure and hospitality
- Education and healthcare
- High-technology and digital economy businesses
- Logistics and warehousing

## Application Process Overview

- **Pre-consultation:** Discuss project concept and incentive options with the CDC or a licensed advisor
- **Feasibility submission:** Submit project proposal, financial projections, and corporate documents
- **CDC review and approval:** Typically 28 working days for a decision
- **Registration letter issuance:** Entitles the investor to import equipment duty-free and begin construction
- **Final registration:** Issued after equipment installation, granting full QIP status
